Door Lockset
Functions & Cores

STANDARD: A156.2 / A156.13
CYLINDERS: A156.30
GRADES: A156.2 / A156.13
ORG: BHMA / ANSI

Lockset functions define the operational behavior of each opening — which side requires a key, which side is always free for egress, and how the latch and deadbolt interact. Functions apply across mortise (A156.13) and cylindrical (A156.2) platforms; exact codes and behavior vary by manufacturer and series. Always verify against manufacturer cut sheets before specifying.

ANSI/BHMA A156.30 · Cylinder Reference

Core & Cylinder Types

Standard Cylinder
Removal: Requires disassembly of lockset or cylinder pull tools. Not field-interchangeable without hardware.

Rekeying: By locksmith on-site or cylinder sent out. Pins replaced in place.

Most common for small-scale projects and budget work. Lower upfront cost; higher long-term cost on large keying systems.
Permanent Low Cost
SFIC — Small Format IC
Removal: Control key removes and inserts core in seconds, without tools or disassembly.

Standard: ANSI/BHMA A156.30 — cross-compatible housing across manufacturers (BEST, Corbin, Arrow, Yale, Medeco, etc.).

Industry standard for large keying systems. Core changes can be delegated to trained facilities staff. 7-pin format.
IC Standard Cross-Brand 7-Pin
LFIC — Large Format IC
Removal: Control key interchangeable, no tools required. Larger housing than SFIC.

Standard: Manufacturer-specific — Schlage FSIC, ASSA ABLOY, Sargent. Not cross-compatible with SFIC housing.

Specify manufacturer at project outset — LFIC cores are not interchangeable between brands. Confirm with hardware distributor.
Mfr-Specific Large Format
Construction Core
Purpose: Temporary cores installed at hardware rough-in, keyed alike across all openings for contractor access during construction.

Removal: Removed and replaced with owner's permanent cores at substantial completion, or upon owner's authorization.

Never include in permanent keying schedule. Collect and destroy construction keys at turnover.
Temporary Keyed Alike Remove at SC
Restricted Keyway
Purpose: Proprietary or licensed keyway with controlled key duplication — keys cannot be copied at a standard hardware store.

Formats: Available in standard (fixed) or IC format. Examples: Medeco, Mul-T-Lock, Schlage Everest, BEST Primus.

Specify when key control is a project requirement — high-security facilities, government, data centers.
High Security Controlled Dup.
ANSI/BHMA · Hardware Grades

Grade Comparison

ANSI/BHMA grades apply across cylindrical locks (A156.2), mortise locks (A156.13), exit devices (A156.3), and closers (A156.4). Always specify Grade 1 for commercial and institutional work. Grade designations are specific to each product standard — a Grade 1 closer is not the same test as a Grade 1 lockset.

Function codes and behaviors vary by manufacturer, hardware series, and platform (mortise vs. cylindrical). ANSI/BHMA A156.13 F-codes apply specifically to mortise locks; cylindrical (A156.2) functions use similar names but different designation systems. For exit device trim functions reference A156.3. Always verify function behavior against manufacturer product data and cut sheets before specifying. Consult a hardware consultant or distributor on projects with complex keying or access control requirements.
