ASTM Standards · Grades & Types

Brick Types & Standards

Standard Description & Grades Applications
ASTM
C216
Face Brick Fired Clay
Face Brick
Most Specified Exposed Masonry Clay / Shale
Grades: SW (Severe Weathering) and MW (Moderate Weathering) — classified by freeze-thaw resistance and water absorption limits.
Types: FBS (standard), FBX (select, tight dimensional tolerance), FBA (architectural, non-uniform texture/color intentional).
Compressive strength: Min. 3,000 psi (MW) to 3,000 psi (SW); absorption limits vary by grade.
Color & texture: Controlled during manufacturing — wire cut, sand-struck, smooth, tumbled, or slurried finishes available.
SW — Severe Weathering MW — Moderate Weathering
Use Where
All exposed exterior masonry in freezing climates (SW) Facades, parapets, chimneys, retaining walls Interior feature walls (MW acceptable) Architectural character — FBA for intentional variation
Specify SW grade for all exterior work in ASTM weathering index regions above 50 (most of northern US). Verify local weathering region before specifying MW.
ASTM
C62
Building Brick Fired Clay
Building (Common) Brick
Backup Wythe Clay / Shale No Appearance Control
Grades: SW, MW, and NW (No Weathering — interior use only).
Difference from C216: No appearance requirements — color, texture, and size variation acceptable. Used where not exposed to view or weather.
Common use: Backup wythes in cavity wall construction, interior grouted cells, infill. Structural performance similar to face brick at lower cost.
Note: Not appropriate where aesthetic consistency matters.
SW MW NW — No Weathering (interior)
Use Where
Backup wythes in cavity wall or veneer construction Structural masonry not exposed to view Below-grade construction (SW grade required) Cost-saving filler in grouted or reinforced masonry
Never specify C62 where the brick will be visible — use C216 FBX or FBS. Below grade, confirm SW grade and bituminous damp-proofing.
ASTM
C652
Hollow Brick Fired Clay
Hollow Brick
Cored Groutable Lighter Weight
Core area: 25–60% of gross cross-sectional area (larger than standard perforations in solid brick).
Grades: SW and MW, same weathering criteria as C216.
Types: HBS (standard), HBX (select), HBA (architectural), HBB (building — no appearance control).
Structural: Cores can be aligned and grouted with rebar for reinforced hollow brick masonry (RHB) — used in seismic zones as an alternative to CMU.
Use Where
Reinforced masonry walls in seismic or high-wind zones Load-bearing brick construction needing rebar Weight-sensitive applications vs solid brick Exposed architectural single-wythe walls
Grout design and consolidation requirements per MSJC (TMS 602). Confirm core alignment and grout lift heights with structural engineer.
ASTM
C902
C1272
Paving Brick Fired Clay
Paving Brick
High Abrasion Resistance Low Absorption Pedestrian & Vehicular
C902: Pedestrian and light traffic paving — three classes (SX, MX, NX) based on exposure; three types (PS, PX, PA) based on appearance control. Min. compressive strength 8,000 psi.
C1272: Heavy vehicular paving brick — higher abrasion resistance and compressive strength requirements (min. 10,000 psi). Use for driveways, loading docks, industrial floors.
Abrasion index: C902 limits measured by ASTM C418 (sand blast) method; C1272 uses C1353 (abrasion resistance of brick).
Use Where
Plazas, walkways, courtyards (C902 SX or MX) Driveways, parking, loading areas (C1272) Historic streetscapes and alleys Interior floors subject to wheeled traffic
Set in dry sand (flexible) or mortar (rigid) bed. Flexible set allows easier repair; rigid required for vehicular loading. Specify slip-resistance per project use.
ASTM
C1405
Glazed Brick Fired Clay
Glazed Brick
Ceramic Glaze Cleanable Healthcare / Labs
Glaze: Ceramic or resinous glaze fused to one or more brick faces during kiln firing — creates a non-porous, smooth, washable surface.
Chemical resistance: Available in acid-resistant grades for laboratory and industrial environments.
Types: Type I (single-face glazed — wall use), Type II (two or more faces — corner, jamb, and return conditions).
Colors: Wide palette; custom colors available with larger minimum orders. Color consistency is tighter than unglazed face brick.
Use Where
Hospital corridors, kitchens, and clinical areas Schools, locker rooms, and public restrooms Industrial facilities — chemical and acid exposure Transit stations and public interior walls
Use non-staining white portland cement mortar — standard mortar can stain glaze. Clean with mild acid wash only if manufacturer-approved. Specify sealant at all movement joints.
ASTM
C55
Concrete Brick Portland Cement
Concrete Brick
Portland Cement Aggregate Lower Cost
Composition: Portland cement, aggregates, and water — molded and cured, not kiln-fired. Dimensional tolerances tighter than fired clay.
Grades: Grade N (general use, exposed or unexposed) and Grade S (unexposed use only).
Shrinkage: Concrete brick shrinks as it cures — control joints are required at closer intervals than clay brick. Movement joint spacing per BIA TN-18A.
Color: Integral pigment; color is consistent and UV-stable but limited palette compared to fired clay. Cannot match the color depth of vitrified clay.
Use Where
Projects where clay brick budget is prohibitive Backup wythes and non-exposed masonry Landscape walls and site masonry Applications requiring very tight dimensional tolerance
Do not mix clay and concrete brick in the same wythe — differential thermal and moisture movement will crack mortar joints. Specify movement joints at max. 20 ft. o.c. horizontally.
ASTM C270 · Mortar for Unit Masonry

Mortar Types

M
2,500 psi min.
Use: Below-grade masonry, retaining walls, foundations, sewers, manholes, and masonry in contact with earth. Highest compressive strength.

High strength makes Type M brittle — not recommended for above-grade exposed brick where some flexibility is needed to accommodate movement.
1 portland : ¼ lime : 3 sand
or 1 portland : 3 sand (no lime)
Below Grade Retaining Walls
S
1,800 psi min.
Use: Above-grade exterior masonry subject to high lateral loads (wind, seismic), at or below grade with SW brick, and structural masonry. Balances strength and bond.

High bond strength improves lateral load transfer at bed joints — preferred for reinforced masonry and areas with significant wind pressure.
1 portland : ½ lime : 4½ sand
or 1 portland : ½ masonry cement : 4½ sand
Structural Masonry High Wind / Seismic
N
750 psi min.
Use: General-purpose above-grade exterior and interior masonry. The most widely used mortar for face brick veneer, parapets, and chimneys.

Lower strength than S or M provides better workability, improved bond, and greater flexibility to accommodate thermal and moisture movement. Preferred by many masons for appearance work.
1 portland : 1 lime : 6 sand
or 1 masonry cement : 3 sand
Most Common Face Brick Veneer
O
350 psi min.
Use: Interior non-load-bearing partitions, and repointing of existing masonry (historic preservation). High lime content — very workable, soft, and forgiving.

Do not use for exterior masonry subject to freezing — water absorption and low strength make Type O vulnerable to freeze-thaw damage. Excellent for repointing soft historic brick where a stiffer mortar would damage units.
1 portland : 2 lime : 9 sand
or 1 masonry cement : 4½ sand
Interior Only Repointing
K
75 psi min.
Use: Historic restoration only — soft, lime-based mortar matched to the original mortar in historic brick masonry. Not in ASTM C270; defined by ASTM C1713 for repointing.

Critical for historic preservation: mortar must always be softer than the brick units. Using Type N or S on historic soft brick will cause spalling — moisture is driven into the unit rather than the sacrificial joint.
1 portland : 3 lime : 10–12 sand
Historic Restoration ASTM C1713
BIA Technical Note 8 · Joint Finishing

Mortar Joint Profiles

Concave
Tooled with a round jointer. Compresses mortar tight against both brick edges — best weather resistance. Standard for all exterior masonry.
Recommended
V-Joint
Sharp V tooled into the face. Compresses mortar at both brick edges; good weather resistance and a crisp shadow line.
Recommended
Flush
Cut or struck flush with a trowel — not tooled or compressed. Less water-resistant; acceptable interior or under plaster and stucco base coats.
Interior / Under Plaster
Weathered
Top edge recedes inward; bottom projects flush with the face. The projecting lower edge deflects water away from the wall. Good weather performance.
Good Weather
Raked
Mortar raked back from the face — creates a strong shadow line. The open recess collects water and ice. Avoid on all exterior masonry in freeze-thaw climates.
Avoid Exterior
Struck
Top edge is flush; bottom slopes inward. The projecting top ledge directs water into the joint rather than away — poor weather performance.
Poor Weather
Bond Patterns · Structural & Decorative

Bond Patterns

Running Bond
Each course offset ½ brick length. The standard bond for veneer and single-wythe walls. Maximum structural efficiency — all stretchers.
Most Common Veneer
Stack Bond
Vertical joints align in all courses. No masonry bond — requires horizontal joint reinforcement at every course or 16″ o.c. max. per code.
Modern Rebar Required
Flemish Bond
Alternating stretcher and header in each course, headers centered on stretchers above and below. Traditional; strong visual pattern. Headers tie wythes together in solid masonry.
Traditional Solid Masonry
English Bond
Alternating courses of stretchers and headers. Strong structural bond for solid masonry; bold horizontal banding. Traditional for load-bearing walls.
Load-Bearing Traditional
Common Bond
Running bond with a header course every 5th or 6th course. Economical version of English bond — structural tie at intervals with faster-laying stretcher courses between.
Economical Solid Masonry
⅓ Running Bond
Each course offset ⅓ brick length. Diagonal joint lines result — commonly used for longer brick (Norman, Roman) where ½ offset looks awkward.
Long Brick Diagonal Lines
Herringbone
Bricks laid at 45° in alternating directions. Used for paving, floor patterns, and decorative wall panels. Not suitable for structural wythe construction.
Paving Decorative
Basket Weave
Pairs of bricks alternating horizontal and vertical. Traditional paving pattern; also used for decorative wall panels and garden paths.
Paving Decorative
Material & Firing · Common Color Ranges

Brick Color & Texture

Red / Salmon
Classic US face brick; high iron oxide content; common in mid-Atlantic and Midwest.
Deep Red / Cordovan
High-fired red clay; rich color with vitrified surface; denser and lower absorption.
Buff / Tan
Low iron; common in Southwest and Great Plains; used for limestone-compatible palettes.
Yellow / Gold
High calcium and magnesium; associated with Midwestern clay deposits.
Gray / Sage
Shale-based or high-firing reduction atmosphere; used in contemporary and industrial palettes.
Charcoal / Black
Manganese or iron-manganese; very high-fired; dense and vitrified. Modern and industrial use.
Blended / Variegated
Mixed lots with intentional color variation — FBA type. Adds depth; traditional and historic character.
White / Cream
Painted, glazed, or light-firing clay. White glazed brick per ASTM C1405; painted requires sealant.
Wire Cut
Column of extruded clay cut with wire — rough, dragged texture. Exposes aggregate. Most common commercial face brick finish.
Sand Struck
Mold-cast with sand release agent; smooth faces with slight irregularity. Traditional; used in historic and residential work.
Smooth / Die Skin
Extruded without wire cutting — very smooth face. Often used for glazed brick base and contemporary clean-line facades.
Tumbled / Handmade
Tumbled after firing for chipped edges and worn appearance. Or hand-molded for authentic historic character. Higher cost and color variability.

For reference only. Sizes and coursing shown are ASTM/BIA standard — verify against manufacturer published sizes before detailing. Mortar type selection depends on weathering region, structural requirements, and existing masonry compatibility. Consult ASTM C270, BIA Technical Notes, and your AHJ before specifying. For historic preservation work, require mortar testing to match existing joint composition per ASTM C1713.
