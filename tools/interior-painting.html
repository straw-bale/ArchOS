Section 02

Paint Systems
by Substrate

Interior · Walls & Ceilings
New Gypsum
Wallboard
1 PVA drywall sealer — 1 coat, full surface
2 Latex finish — 2 coats
Never skip primer on new GWB. Unprimed paper face absorbs paint unevenly, causing "flashing" — dull patches where suction varied. PVA sealer is mandatory, not optional.
Interior · Repaint
Existing Painted
Surfaces
1 Wash surface; fill cracks and holes; sand repairs smooth
2 Spot prime all patched areas
3 1–2 finish coats (full prime coat if significant color change)
When applying lower sheen over higher sheen (e.g. flat over semi-gloss), degloss or apply a full prime coat over the entire surface — finish paint will not bond reliably to high-gloss substrates.
Interior · Walls
New Plaster
1 Allow full cure — minimum 30 days; 60+ days for high-lime plaster
2 Alkali-resistant latex primer — 1 coat
3 Latex finish — 2 coats
The high pH of fresh plaster degrades standard paint film. Test with litmus paper before priming — pH >9 requires additional cure time. Alkali-resistant primer is mandatory, not a substitute for adequate cure.
Interior · Trim
Wood Trim
(Painted Finish)
1 Sand 120–150 grit; fill nail holes and open grain
2 Alkyd or latex wood primer — 1 coat
3 Semi-gloss or gloss finish — 2 coats; sand lightly between with 220 grit
Back-prime all six faces of trim before installation — painting only the face traps moisture, leading to cupping, grain raise, and paint failure at edges and back face.
Interior · Masonry
Concrete / CMU
1 Clean surface; remove efflorescence; patch spalls and voids
2 Heavy-bodied block filler — fills open face texture
3 Latex finish (eggshell or satin typical) — 2 coats
Block filler is not optional — standard primer cannot bridge the open CMU face texture. Use exterior-grade block filler for below-grade or moisture-exposed masonry.
Interior · Metal Doors & Frames
Hollow Metal Doors
& Frames
1 Verify factory primer — most HM ships with gray alkyd or epoxy shop primer already applied
2 Spot prime any abraded or unprimed areas (field cuts, welds) with rust-inhibitive primer
3 DTM latex or alkyd semi-gloss (G6) — 2 coats over factory primer; confirm compatibility with shop primer type
Do not specify standard wall paint on metal doors and frames — use a DTM or enamel product rated for metal substrates. For galvanized frames, use galvanized-metal primer — standard rust-inhibitive primers do not bond to zinc. Coordinate finish color with hardware schedule. Common products: SW ProIndustrial DTM, PPG PITT-TECH Plus, BM Acrylic DTM Enamel. For institutional high-abuse applications (schools, hospitals, correctional), BM Pre-Catalyzed Waterborne Epoxy provides superior hardness and chemical resistance.
Section 03

VOC Limits &
Sustainability Certifications

Regulations · VOC Limits
VOC Compliance
EPA Architectural Rule: Flat: ≤50 g/L; Non-flat: ≤150 g/L. Federal minimums — local rules are stricter.
SCAQMD South Coast (CA): Flat: ≤50 g/L; Non-flat: ≤100 g/L. One of the strictest U.S. limits — confirm if project is in an OTC or SCAQMD jurisdiction.
Zero VOC Products labeled "Zero VOC" must be ≤5 g/L per EPA. Tinted versions may exceed this — always verify tinted VOC with the manufacturer's data sheet, not just the base paint.
VOC limits apply to the coating as applied (including tint). Verify the tinted product VOC — many "Zero VOC" bases exceed limits once colorant is added. Obtain Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for all products.
Green Building · Certifications
LEED · WELL · Declare
LEED EQ Credit — Low-Emitting Materials: Paints must meet SCAQMD Rule 1113 or Green Seal GS-11 limits. Applies to wet-applied products installed inside the waterproofing layer. Document VOC content at time of installation.
WELL Feature X5 — Material Restrictions: Limits VOCs in wet-applied products. Aligns closely with LEED EQ requirements. Green Seal GS-11 or GREENGUARD Gold certification is typically accepted as compliance evidence.
Declare Living Building Challenge (ILFI): Declare labels disclose full ingredient transparency. Avoid Red List chemicals — many solvent-based and alkyd paints contain compounds on the Red List. Prefer water-based latex with Declare or HPD documentation.
GREENGUARD UL GREENGUARD Gold (formerly Children & Schools) is the most commonly accepted third-party VOC certification for paint. SW and PPG both publish GREENGUARD-certified product lines — confirm the specific product is listed.
For health-sensitive occupancies (schools, healthcare, childcare), specify GREENGUARD Gold certified products at minimum. For Living Building Challenge projects, request HPDs (Health Product Declarations) and avoid alkyd/solvent-based products where possible.
Section 04

Glossary
Common Paint Terms

Latex
Water-based paint using acrylic or vinyl-acrylic binders. Fast dry time, low odor, easy cleanup, and lower VOCs than alkyd. The dominant binder type for commercial interior painting. Performance has largely matched alkyd for most interior applications. Sometimes called "waterborne" or "acrylic latex."
Alkyd
Oil-based paint using alkyd resin binders derived from oil and alcohol. Historically preferred for trim and metal for its hard, durable film. Higher VOCs, longer dry time, requires solvent cleanup. Increasingly replaced by waterborne alkyd formulas that use water as carrier but retain alkyd's hard finish. Often called "oil-based" in the field.
DTM — Direct to Metal
A paint formulated to bond directly to bare or primed metal without a separate primer coat. Contains corrosion inhibitors and adhesion promoters in the finish coat. Used on structural steel, hollow metal doors and frames, pipes, and miscellaneous metals. Examples: SW ProIndustrial DTM, PPG PITT-TECH Plus.
VOC — Volatile Organic Compound
Organic chemicals that evaporate at room temperature, contributing to indoor air quality issues and smog formation. In paint, VOCs are released during application and drying. Measured in grams per liter (g/L). Regulated by EPA, SCAQMD, and required for LEED and WELL certification compliance.
MPI — Master Painters Institute
Industry organization that maintains the Approved Products List (APL) — a catalog of tested and certified paint products referenced by MPI number in commercial specifications. MPI gloss levels (G1–G7) are the standard sheen classification used in specs. CSI MasterSpec and most commercial painting specs (Section 09 91 00) reference MPI.
PVA Sealer
Polyvinyl acetate drywall primer/sealer. Applied to new gypsum wallboard before finish coats to seal the paper face and equalize suction across the surface. Without it, finish paint absorbs unevenly into the paper and compound, causing "flashing." Not interchangeable with general-purpose latex primer.
Dry Film Thickness (DFT)
The thickness of a paint film after solvent has evaporated, measured in mils (thousandths of an inch). Critical for protective coatings on metal — insufficient DFT reduces corrosion resistance. For standard interior latex, DFT is less critical but affects hide and durability. Verify with manufacturer data sheets when writing a tight spec.
Enamel
Originally referred to oil-based gloss paints that dry to a hard, smooth film. Today "enamel" is used loosely for any hard-finish paint in semi-gloss or gloss, including waterborne alkyd enamels. In specs, "alkyd enamel" typically means a hard, oil-based trim paint; "latex enamel" means a hard-film water-based alternative. Verify the binder type when reviewing product submittals.
Epoxy / Pre-Catalyzed Waterborne Epoxy
Two-component or pre-catalyzed coating that cures to an exceptionally hard, chemical-resistant film. Used in institutional settings (hospitals, schools, correctional), commercial kitchens, locker rooms, and other high-abuse environments. "Pre-catalyzed" means the hardener is already mixed in, simplifying application. Higher cost and more demanding application than standard latex — but significantly more durable. Benjamin Moore's Pre-Catalyzed Waterborne Epoxy is a commonly specified product in this category.
Flashing
Uneven sheen or color variation in a dried paint film, typically caused by differential suction across the substrate. Common on new drywall that wasn't primed — areas over tape joints absorb less paint than areas over raw paper, creating visible patches. Prevented by proper priming. Also caused by touching up flat paint with a different batch or applicator.

For reference only. MPI gloss levels (G1–G7) are industry-standard designations; individual paint product MPI catalog numbers must be verified against the current MPI Approved Products List. Confirm VOC limits comply with local air quality regulations (SCAQMD, OTC, etc.) and project sustainability requirements. Surface preparation standards reference SSPC (now AMPP). Verify all specifications with manufacturer documentation before applying to a project.
